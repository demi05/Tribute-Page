Queen Elizabeth II

Britain's longest living female monarch!

Major events of Queen Elizabeth II's life:

  • Birth- Born Elizabeth Alexandra Mary, Princess of York on 21 April 1926 to her parents, Duke and Duchess of York who would later become King George VI and Queen Elizabeth the Queen Mother. She was Queen of the United Kingdom and other Commonwealth realms from 6 February 1952 until her death in 2022.
  • Princess Margaret- She was joined by a sister, Princess Margaret. She became Countess of Snowdon after marrying her husband, Antony Armstrong-Jones whom the Queen made Earl of Snowdon. They divorced in 1978. Margaret was a controversial member of the British royal family. Her divorce received much negative publicity, and her private life was for many years the subject of intense speculation by media and royal-watchers. Her health gradually deteriorated in the final two decades of her life. She was a heavy smoker for most of her adult life, and had a lung operation in 1985, a bout of pneumonia in 1993, and at least three strokes between 1998 and 2001. She died in London on 9 February 2002, after suffering a fourth and final stroke at the age of 71.
  • Marriage- She got married to Prince Phillip (born Prince Philip of Greece and Denmark) on 20th November, 1947 after he returned from the war. He was later created Duke of Edinburgh on the day of their wedding. Philip had first met the then Princess Elizabeth in 1934. In July 1939, he began corresponding with the 13-year-old Princess Elizabeth.
  • Ascension to the throne- When her father died in February 1952, Elizabeth—then 25 years old—became queen of seven independent Commonwealth countries: the United Kingdom, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, South Africa, Pakistan, and Ceylon (known today as Sri Lanka), as well as Head of the Commonwealth. At the time, the then-Princess Elizabeth was on a royal tour in Kenya with Prince Philip.
  • Coronation- She was officialy crowned Queen Elizabeth II on 2nd June 1953.
  • Issue- She had 4 children; Charles, Prince of Wales now King Charles III, born 14 November 1948; Anne, Princess Royal born 15 August 1950, Andrew, Duke of York born 19 February 1960 and Edward, Earl of Wessexx born 10 March 1964.
  • Investiture of the Prince of Wales- In 1958, she invested the new Prince of Wales, her son Prince Charles
  • Countries seeking indepedence- Many countries started to seek their independence from Britian in the 1960's and 70's
  • annus horribilis- The expression was brought to modern prominence by Queen Elizabeth II. In a speech at Guildhall on 24 November 1992, marking her Ruby Jubilee on the throne, she said: 1992 is not a year on which I shall look back with undiluted pleasure. In the words of one of my more sympathetic correspondents, it has turned out to be an annus horribilis. Many unpleasant events happened to the royal family that year. You can read more on that here.
  • Death of Prince Phillip- Philip is the longest-lived male member of the British royal family. He retired from his royal duties on 2 August 2017, aged 96, having completed 22,219 solo engagements and 5,493 speeches from 1952. On the 9th of April 2021, Prince Phillip died at the age of 99.
  • Reign- Elizabeth reigned as a constitutional monarch through major political changes such as the Troubles in Northern Ireland, devolution in the United Kingdom, the decolonisation of Africa, and the United Kingdom's accession to the European Communities and withdrawal from the European Union. The number of her realms varied over time as territories gained independence and some realms became republics. Her many historic visits and meetings include state visits to China in 1986, Russia in 1994, and the Republic of Ireland in 2011, and meetings with five popes. In February 2022, the Queen celebrated her 70th year as the monarch of Britian. Her reign of 70 years and 214 days is the longest of any British monarch, the longest recorded of any female head of state in history, and the second-longest verified reign of any monarch in history. She was queen regnant of 32 sovereign states during her lifetime and 15 at the time of her death.
  • Death- On September 8 2022,she died at age 96 in her Balmoral Castle, Aberdeenshire, in September 2022, and was succeeded by her eldest son, Charles III.. She was buried on the 19th of September 2022 at King George VI Memorial Chapel, St George's Chapel, Windsor Castle. Her state funeral was the first to be held in the United Kingdom since that of Winston Churchill in 1965.
